UX Design Project Documentation

This page documents the UX work used to redesign the Taniti website from a jumbled experience into a structured Discover, Plan, Book flow.

A. Project Timeline: 4-Week Schedule

WeekPhaseKey Activities
Week 1Discovery & ResearchAnalyze survey data, define user personas, perform competitive analysis of island tourism sites, and map the user journey.
Week 2Information ArchitectureCreate a sitemap to fix the jumbled navigation, define content hierarchy, and develop low-fidelity wireframes for core pages.
Week 3Design & PrototypingBuild high-fidelity interactive prototypes, integrate booking flow simulations, and ensure mobile responsiveness.
Week 4Testing & IterationConduct guerrilla usability testing, analyze qualitative feedback, finalize design adjustments, and prepare hand-off documentation.

B. User Persona Profile

Sarah Miller

Age: 42
Occupation: Marketing Manager
Location: Chicago, USA
Annual Income: $75,000

Background

Sarah is planning a 5-to-6-day vacation for her family of four. This is their first time visiting Taniti. They are arriving via cruise ship but staying in a hotel to explore the island further.

Goals

  • Find safe, family-friendly activities like snorkeling and the local history museum.
  • Understand hotel shuttles and walking in Taniti City.
  • Confirm U.S. Dollars and 120v outlets.

Frustrations

  • Outdated websites that do not work well on phones.
  • Unclear booking information for rainforest and volcano tours.
  • Concerns about rural language barriers and pickpocketing.

D. Guerrilla Usability Testing

Feedback ReceivedParticipantActionable?Relevance
“I couldn't find the drinking age or power outlet info easily.”Debra JanulisYesHigh. This was buried in a general FAQ and needs a Quick Facts sidebar.
“The Book Now button for the volcano tour was too small on my phone.”Joel JanulisYesHigh. Relates to mobile compatibility.
“I wish there were more photos of the local food.”Haily WinelandNoLow. This is a content/asset issue, not a structural UX issue for this phase.
“The transportation page is helpful, but I need to know if I can use my credit card for the bus.”Stephen JanulisYesHigh. Added a note regarding currency and credit-card acceptance.

Feedback Incorporation

The design now includes a Traveler’s Essentials sticky sidebar on desktop and standard stacked content on mobile. It provides quick access to currency, power outlets, safety tips, language notes, and bus payment guidance. Primary CTA buttons were increased to at least 48x48 pixels to improve mobile usability.
